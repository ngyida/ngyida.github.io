SWE (intern) at Ryde

May 2023 - Oct 2023

Worked on internal tools

Context

This was my first SWE internship, during my second year of university.

I joined the technology team behind the ride-hailing platform where I worked on the internal systems used by the teams around it.

My work

My job was basically to reduce as many pain points as possible for teams across the company, including marketing, operations, and customer service.

Most of that happened through internal automations and tooling features. The work was fairly request-driven and all over the place in the best possible way. A few of my favorite projects were:

  • RBAC model for better authorization management on the system
  • remote configuration so non-technical teams could publish changes to the company app
  • geographical service controls to support compliance requirements
  • a revamp of the data access system for better UI, UX, and performance, which led to a 60% KPI improvement for the customer service team

My takeaways

Being my first SWE experience, I joined with full confidence, got humbled, and then left with a much stronger sense that this was work I wanted to keep doing.

It served as a very good introduction to engineering and made the work feel real to me. I could often see the operational annoyance first, build something to reduce it, and then watch another team feel the difference.

SWE (full-time) at ByteDance

July 2025 - Present

Worked on distributed authorization systems

Context

This was my first full-time role out of university

My team builds a distributed authorization platform that spans close to a hundred regions. It serves major ByteDance product lines such as TikTok, Volcano Engine, and SEED.

It dropped me straight into a problem space that I enjoy a lot: a system with large scale, real constraints, and very little room for hand-wavy performance. Afterall, authorization sits critically on every execution path.

My work

I worked on a bunch of things, but these were probably my three favorite projects, each with a different flavor of systems problem.

1. Engineered a new powerful ABAC model

  • designed and integrated a new ABAC model alongside the existing RBAC and PBAC models
  • unlocked a new dimension of the system’s expressiveness and brought in customers which the older models couldn’t support

I liked this one because it was not just about adding a feature. It was about extending the core system in a way that stayed compatible, which is usually the harder part. This scales easily with new customers, and new features become by-products of the new model.

2. Led an initiative to improve authorization explainability

I introduced two new approaches to make authorization decisions easier to understand:

  • visibility into full authorization graphs, useful for debugging and root cause analysis
  • reverse-abstraction so customers could reason about authorization using their own business concepts

My initivatives turned into actual tools that enhanced the customers’ understanding of our system. More importantly, customers are empowered to self-help and incident debugging are minimized for the team.

3. Built a high-performance, low-latency authorization SDK

  • removed network I/O from the critical path by serving authorization locally
  • tackled high memory usage with techniques such as data compression and data structures like roaring bitmaps
  • built the surrounding architecture for a hybrid push-pull caching mechanism
  • achieved an average latency of 0.08ms on authorization checks

This one was satisfying because it had the technical full package: latency constraints, memory tradeoffs, caching design, and the kind of engineering work where every decision leaks into another one.

My takeaways

This role pushed me to think about problem-solving as something much broader than implementation.

Technical competency still matters a lot, obviously. But the more interesting part of engineering is usually earlier: identifying the actual problem, understanding the constraints properly, exploring different ways to attack it, and only then committing to a solution.

I think this role also made me appreciate that impact often comes from choosing the right problem to solve, not just solving a chosen problem quickly.

For now, I am mostly just excited to keep showing up, finding sharper problems, and solving them as cleanly as I can.

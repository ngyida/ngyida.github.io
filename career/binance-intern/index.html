SWE (intern) at Binance

Apr 2024 - Aug 2024

Worked on anomaly detection system

Context

This was an internship in my third year of university.

I joined a team building an ML-driven anomaly detection system and worked in an environment where speed matters greatly.

Most of my work was on the backend infrastructure around the detection engine.

My work

I ended up working on many meaningful projects during a relatively short stint:

  • a root cause analysis system supporting more than 50 microservices, significantly reduced the MTTR for incident managers by over 40%
  • a real-time knowledge base for LLM tools to support time-sensitive Web3 domain data & internal workflow
  • an alert aggregation mechanism to reduce alert fatigue
  • a better feedback mechanism aimed at improving the accuracy of the anomaly detection engine

My takeaways

My biggest takeaway was learning that delivering fast is a real skill, and not just a matter of moving hurriedly. For me, it mostly meant understanding requirements accurately, building incrementally, and putting enough guardrails in place that speed did not immediately turn into regressions.

I also really enjoyed working directly with the actual users of the system, especially incident managers handling anomalies in real time. That kind of feedback loop felt very different from working only through product managers or more indirect stakeholders.

It taught me that user communication changes a lot depending on who the user is. Operators tend to care less about polished narratives and more about whether the tool is clear, fast, and reliable when things are already going badly. It forced me to think beyond “can this be built?” and more in terms of “will this actually help when people are under pressure?”. That tends to be a much better filter.

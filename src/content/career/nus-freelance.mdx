## context

This was a short freelance stint on [Orbital](https://www.comp.nus.edu.sg/orbital/), the NUS module where students build independent software projects in teams.

The module had long used an academic portal called Skylab, and I joined a small team of freelance engineers working on Skylab V2.

What made this one fun was that I was not just asked to implement a predefined feature. I got to propose the feature myself, then carry it all the way through.

## my work

I built a feature aimed at improving the mentor-student matching flow.

The idea was to let student teams and mentors browse profiles, express interest, and participate more actively in a multi-round matching process. Put less formally: it was a dating-app-style matching flow, but for academics.

I was the sole engineer for the feature from end to end, covering design, implementation, and deployment.

## my takeaways

This project made me realize how fulfilling it is to build something that begins as your own proposal.

It gave me a rare kind of ownership and there is something especially satisfying about seeing an idea start as "I think this would make the system better" and end up as a real shipped feature. Getting paid was definitely a plus as well.

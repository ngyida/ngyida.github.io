## Context

My first full time job out of university.

I joined a team that builds a global authorization system. 
Our system spans nearly a hundred regions around the world, offering multiple authorization models and capability that serves major Bytedance product lines such as Tiktok, Volcano Engine & SEED. 

## My work

Worked on many things, below are my top 3 favourite projects:

### 1. Engineered a new powerful ABAC model 

- smoothly integrated with the existing RBAC & PBAC model
- unlocked a whole new dimension of authorization that led to the onboarding of new customers whose requirements were previously not met by older models

### 2. Drove an initiation to improve explainability of authorization

- innovated 2 new approachs to improve explainability: 
    - provide visibility of complex authorization graph that can be leveraged for root cause analysis
    - regain business semantics of our customers through reverse-abstraction features. as a resourec agnostic platform, the system is previously unaware of platform's business semantics and often caused confusion within end-users.

### 3. Built a high-performance, low-latency authorization SDK

- solved the bottleneck of network I/O by serving authorization locally
- built supporting architectures to achieve a hybrid push-pull caching mechanism for the SDK
- achieved an average latency of 0.08ms on authorization checks

## My takeaways

Problem-solving goes way beyond implementation. Dont get me wrong, technical competency is still the foundation for engineers. 
However, real problem-solving is carried out when one actively identifies a problem, explore various approaches, before committing to engineering the solution.
The harder the problem you solved, the higher your impact is as an engineer.
Right now, I am just excited to go into office and find new problems, then solve them as elegantly as possible.

## context

My first SWE internship, done in the second-year of my undergrad.

I joined the technology team that develops and maintain the full ride-hailing service app.

## what I built

I was tasked to solve as many painpoints as possible for the other teams in the company, such as the marketing team, operational team, and customer service team.
This was done through developing automations and features on the internal tooling system. 
Projects were pretty much random and dependent on user requests, listed my favourites below:

- RBAC model for the system
- remote configuration for non-technical team to publish changes onto the company app
- geographical service controls to support compliance requirements
- revamped the data-access system for better UI/UX & performance, saw 60% KPI improvement in customer service team

## what I learned

Being my first ever SWE experience, I joined with full confidence, got humbled, then left with a burning passion.

On a serious note, this internship kickstarted my SWE journey.
The most valuable takeaway: I learned what professional engineering looks like now and I think I like it quite a bit.

## context

Internal tooling can look unglamorous from the outside, but it often contains some of the clearest engineering leverage. This role made that very tangible because the systems were used daily by teams whose efficiency depended on them.

## what I built

I worked on a central data-access system and a series of adjacent internal features, including:

- workflow support used by multiple departments
- access-control improvements in the content management system
- remote configuration capability for non-technical teams
- geographical service controls to support compliance requirements

## what mattered technically

The strongest lesson here was that internal tools succeed when they remove ambiguity. A feature that slightly clarifies permissions, reduces manual coordination, or aligns data across teams can have outsized value even if the underlying technology is straightforward.

## what I learned

I came away with a durable appreciation for software that quietly improves an organization from the inside. It also pushed me toward backend and platform work where the user is often another team rather than the public internet.

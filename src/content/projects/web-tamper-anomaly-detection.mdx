## context

This project sat at a point I find especially compelling: security-oriented behavior analysis with a strong need for interpretability. Detecting something unusual is useful, but in practice teams also need to know why a signal deserves attention.

## what I built

The project focused on detecting anomalies caused by web tamper attacks and exploring ways to explain those anomalies in a form that was useful to humans reviewing the results.

## what mattered technically

The interesting challenge was balancing detection quality with explanation quality. A system that flags the right behavior but cannot support investigation is incomplete. That made representation, prompting, and output framing just as important as the raw anomaly signal.

## what I learned

This project reinforced my interest in security-adjacent backend and applied intelligence problems, especially where the end goal is not just prediction but decision support.

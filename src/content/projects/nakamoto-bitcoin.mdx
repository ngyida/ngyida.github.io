## context

I wanted a project that made distributed systems ideas feel less abstract. Bitcoin-style systems are conceptually rich, but they become much easier to understand when you try to implement their moving parts yourself.

## what I built

This project was a command-line application that modeled a simplified Nakamoto Bitcoin network.

## what mattered technically

The value was in translating protocol-level ideas into working behavior. Building even a simplified version forced me to think more clearly about coordination, state propagation, and the assumptions that make decentralized systems function.

## what I learned

This was one of the projects that deepened my taste for systems work. It made the theoretical side of distributed computing feel much more tangible.

I like work that is technically sharp but still grounded in how people actually operate. In practice, that usually means backend systems, internal platforms, security infrastructure, or operational tooling where design decisions show up quickly in someone else's workflow.

I am drawn to teams that combine engineering depth with a healthy sense of pragmatism. I care a lot about clear ownership, thoughtful iteration, and building systems that reduce confusion rather than simply adding complexity in impressive ways.

The entries below are not just a timeline. Each one is a deeper note about a period of work: what the problem looked like, what I built, what mattered technically, and what I learned from it.

Projects are usually where I test ideas, tools, or technical instincts.

Most of the time, I follow the idea far enough to understand where the technical tension actually lives. Sometimes that produces something polished. Other times it produces a useful mess, which is still a pretty good outcome by project standards.

That is roughly the difference between the two sections below.

The cool projects are the ones that feel more complete, more substantial, or more worth showing off without apology. The not-cool ones are usually experiments, partial explorations, or things that taught me something before they had the chance to become properly impressive.
